Majors Baseball

Ages 11-12

You will be amazed at the caliber of baseball played at this level! The game remains at a 46’ pitching mound and 60’ base path but some of these players can now pitch in excess of 60 miles per hour. Base stealing is allowed (no lead offs). Umpires are provided for each game. Both boys and girls aged 11 and 12 are welcome at this level, with the occasional skilled 10 year-old.

The Majors Division has a tournament team which will play in the District 7 (city-wide) Championships during the second week of July. The winner of that moves on to Provincials. The winner of Provincials moves on to Canadians. The winner of Canadians moves on to the Little League World Series in Williamsport, PA, USA!

Locations

Practices occur at Hollywood Park and the batting cage. House league and home games are played at Hollywood and you may travel within Victoria a handful of times during the season for intelock games.

Time Commitment

Majors baseball requires tremendous player and parental commitment. The regular season runs from early April to mid-June with tournament play often extending into the first half of summer.

Players can expect to practice twice a week and play 1-3 games each week. Game times are variable as our teams will interlock with other Victoria teams.

Team Selection

Player assessments for Majors Baseball are held in March. Teams are then drafted with an emphasis on achieving balanced teams. The draft makes it difficult for us to accommodate friend requests at this level.

Equipment & Apparel

Players must have a glove, batting helmet, grey baseball pants, and wear pelvic protection (jock/jill).
Plastic cleats are required. Metal cleats are not allowed.
Bats are supplied by the team. Players may use their own bat as long as it has the USA Baseball™ stamp, signifying it's allowed for Little League play. Batting gloves are optional.
Each player will receive a Beacon Hill Little League team jersey, long-sleeved shirt, socks, and hat. The jersey must be returned at the end of the season.
